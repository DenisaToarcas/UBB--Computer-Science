What is Sustainability?

Sustainability focuses on meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their needs. It encompasses three main pillars: economic, environmental, and socialâ€”also known informally as profits, planet, and people.


Ways to Live Sustainably

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle: Minimize waste, use products multiple times, and recycle items whenever possible.

Conserve Water: Fix leaking taps, use water-saving appliances, and collect rainwater for garden use.

Save Energy: Use energy-efficient appliances, switch to renewable energy sources, and improve home insulation.

Type of Energy Benefits Challenges
Solar Power Abundant, sustainable, reduces electricity bills, low maintenance costs Weather dependent, requires space for installation, high initial cost
Wind Power Cost-effective, scalable, clean fuel source, low operational costs Site specific, noise and visual pollution, threat to wildlife
Hydroelectric Power Highly efficient, consistent, storage capabilities, flexible Environmental impact, displacement, high upfront building costs
Geothermal Energy Low emissions, reliable, small footprint, stable Location specific, high initial costs, potential for groundwater contamination
Biomass Energy Renewable, can help reduce waste, can be used to produce different forms of energy Can contribute to deforestation, high costs of production, competition with food production
